## Plain English Summary

ACOP L8 is the Health and Safety Executive Approved Code of Practice for controlling Legionella bacteria in water systems. It supports legal duties under the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 and COSHH.

For FM, Legionella control means knowing the water system, assessing risk, controlling temperature, flushing low-use outlets, maintaining records and acting quickly when control limits fail.

## Why It Matters in Facilities Management

Legionnaires' disease can be fatal. Buildings with stored water, calorifiers, showers, dead legs, low-use outlets, complex pipework, spa pools, cooling systems or vulnerable users require serious water hygiene management.

A Facilities Manager must be able to prove that the water system is understood, monitored and controlled.

## Who Has Duties

- Employers.
- People in control of premises.
- Dutyholders responsible for water systems.
- Appointed Responsible Persons.
- Contractors managing water hygiene tasks.

## What Good Control Requires

A compliant water hygiene system should include:

- Legionella risk assessment;
- written control scheme;
- named Responsible Person;
- schematic drawings where appropriate;
- hot and cold water temperature monitoring;
- flushing regime for low-use outlets;
- inspection and cleaning of tanks where relevant;
- calorifier checks where relevant;
- shower head cleaning/descaling;
- contractor competence checks;
- clear escalation when control limits fail.

## FM Actions

A Facilities Manager should:

- confirm there is a current Legionella risk assessment;
- maintain a water hygiene logbook;
- verify routine monitoring is completed;
- investigate missed checks;
- act on out-of-range temperatures;
- remove or manage dead legs and low-use outlets;
- ensure water hygiene contractors are competent;
- keep evidence of remedial works;
- review the scheme after changes to the water system.

## Evidence to Keep

- Legionella risk assessment.
- Written scheme of control.
- Responsible Person appointment.
- Temperature logs.
- Flushing records.
- Shower cleaning/descaling records.
- Tank inspection records.
- Calorifier inspection and temperature records.
- Microbiological sampling results where applicable.
- Remedial action records.
- Contractor reports.
- Training records.

## Review Frequency

The exact regime depends on the water system and risk assessment. Typical controls include:

- routine hot and cold temperature monitoring;
- routine flushing for low-use outlets;
- periodic shower head cleaning and descaling;
- periodic tank inspection where relevant;
- risk assessment review after system changes, control failures, occupancy changes or where it may no longer be valid.

## Red Flags

- No named Responsible Person.
- No current risk assessment.
- Missing temperature logs.
- Out-of-range temperatures with no action.
- Low-use outlets not flushed.
- Dead legs not identified.
- Contractor reports not reviewed.
- Care or vulnerable-user settings treated as low risk without justification.

## Interview Talking Points

Use this phrase:

> I manage Legionella through a risk assessment, written control scheme, named ownership, routine monitoring and fast escalation when temperatures or usage patterns fall outside control.
