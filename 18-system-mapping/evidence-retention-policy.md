## Plain English Summary

Evidence retention is the rule for how long FM compliance records must be kept, who owns them, where they are stored and when they can be archived or deleted.

This policy gives FM Control Hub and Compliance Hub a practical retention baseline for UK Facilities Management records. It is not a legal advice substitute; where contracts, insurers, claims, investigations or sector rules require longer retention, keep the record longer.

## Why It Matters in Facilities Management

FM compliance depends on evidence. If a fire alarm fault, asbestos incident, water hygiene failure or contractor injury is questioned later, the organisation must be able to show what was known, what was done and who made the decision.

Poor retention creates two risks:

- evidence is deleted too early and compliance cannot be proved;
- records are kept without control, increasing data protection and retrieval risk.

## Core Principles

- Keep statutory, safety-critical and exposure records for the required minimum period or longer where risk justifies it.
- Keep current live compliance evidence until superseded and then archive it.
- Link records to site, asset, contractor, incident or task.
- Do not delete records under active investigation, claim, enforcement, audit or dispute.
- Separate personal medical information from ordinary FM records.
- Store immutable evidence and record any superseding upload.
- Review retention annually.

## Retention Schedule

| Record type | Minimum retention baseline | Practical FM note |
|---|---:|---|
| Fire risk assessments | Current plus previous versions for at least 6 years | Keep longer for complex sites or claims risk. |
| Fire alarm tests and servicing | 6 years | Keep defects and remedials with service evidence. |
| Emergency lighting records | 6 years | Keep annual duration test evidence separately flagged. |
| Fire door inspections | 6 years | Keep high-risk defects with closure photos. |
| Legionella risk assessments and written schemes | Current plus at least 6 years | Keep historic versions where exposure or illness is alleged. |
| Water hygiene monitoring logs | At least 5 years | Keep longer for healthcare/vulnerable settings or investigations. |
| Asbestos register and management plan | Life of premises control, plus archive | Do not delete historic asbestos information while building risk remains relevant. |
| Asbestos exposure/health records | 40 years where applicable | Keep separately with occupational health controls. |
| COSHH assessments | Current plus 6 years | Keep superseded assessments where exposure may be relevant. |
| COSHH health surveillance records | 40 years where applicable | Health record retention follows HSE guidance and relevant regulations. |
| EICR and electrical remedials | Current cycle plus previous report, minimum 6 years | Keep remedial certificates with observations. |
| PAT/portable equipment checks | Current plus 3 years | Keep longer for incident-related equipment. |
| LOLER thorough examination reports | Life of equipment while in use plus 2 years after disposal as a baseline | Keep defect history for critical lifts/equipment. |
| PUWER inspection/maintenance records | Current plus 6 years | Keep training and defect evidence. |
| Work at height equipment inspections | Current plus 3 years | Keep incident-related records longer. |
| Contractor approval records | Active relationship plus 6 years | Include insurance and competence snapshots. |
| RAMS and permits | 6 years | Keep longer for high-risk works, incidents or claims. |
| Accident and incident records | At least 3 years for RIDDOR/reportable records | Keep longer for serious injury, claims or minors. |
| RIDDOR submissions | At least 3 years | Keep PDF/reference and internal investigation. |
| Waste transfer notes | 2 years | GOV.UK requires paper or electronic copy retention. |
| Hazardous waste consignment notes | At least 3 years | Keep producer copies and disposal chain evidence. |
| Training records | Employment plus 6 years as a practical baseline | Keep statutory/competence training longer where linked to exposure or incidents. |
| Audit reports and findings | 6 years | Keep closure evidence with findings. |
| Board compliance reports | 6 years | Keep risk acceptance and budget decisions. |

## FM Actions

A Facilities Manager should:

- assign each evidence type a retention category;
- store evidence in the correct site, asset, contractor or incident record;
- stop deletion where a claim, investigation, enforcement action or audit is open;
- keep high-risk exposure records under stricter controls;
- review evidence completeness during monthly compliance reporting;
- export evidence packs before contractor portal access ends;
- ensure deleted records have passed retention review and approval.

## Evidence to Keep

- Retention schedule.
- Retention review logs.
- Evidence deletion approvals.
- Legal hold records.
- Data export records.
- Superseded evidence links.
- Access permission reviews.
- Audit trail of evidence uploads and changes.

## Review Frequency

- Policy review: annually.
- Retention schedule review: after legal, insurance, contractual or operational changes.
- Legal hold review: monthly while active.
- Evidence sampling: monthly for high-risk compliance areas and annually across all areas.

## Red Flags

- Contractor portals are treated as the only evidence store.
- Evidence is deleted when a contract ends.
- Defects are hidden inside reports and not retained as actions.
- Health surveillance records are mixed with general FM files.
- Waste transfer notes cannot be produced.
- Old asbestos surveys are deleted after a new survey.
- Dashboard status cannot be traced to retained evidence.

## Longleat / Visitor Attraction Relevance

A visitor attraction may need evidence long after a busy day, event, outage, injury, enclosure defect or contractor incident. High public footfall, animal welfare interfaces, estate infrastructure and media exposure justify disciplined retention.

Longleat-style operations should keep board decisions, temporary controls, incident logs, contractor instructions and closure evidence together so future reviews can understand the decision trail.
