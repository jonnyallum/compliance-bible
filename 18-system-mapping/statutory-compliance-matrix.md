# Statutory Compliance Matrix

## Plain English Summary

A statutory compliance matrix turns legal and good-practice duties into a practical FM schedule. It shows what must be checked, how often, who owns it, what evidence is required and what should happen when something fails.

This matrix is designed for FM Control Hub, Compliance Hub and operational FM teams who need a single view of critical compliance controls.

## Why It Matters in Facilities Management

FM compliance often fails because evidence is scattered across logbooks, contractor portals, inboxes and spreadsheets. A matrix creates one control language for statutory tasks, inspection frequencies, evidence uploads, escalation and audit readiness.

It also helps senior managers understand which failures are life-safety, legal, reputational or operational risks.

## Core Principles

- Separate testing, servicing, inspection, assessment and remedial action.
- Record the source of the duty or authoritative guidance.
- Assign a named owner for each compliance line.
- Store evidence against the asset, site and period.
- Use risk-based frequencies where the law or guidance allows flexibility.
- Escalate missed life-safety tasks immediately.
- Track defects separately from completed inspections.
- Review the matrix after legal, asset, occupancy or operational changes.

## Step-by-Step Process

1. List the compliance area and control activity.
2. Define the normal frequency and whether it is statutory, guidance-based or risk-based.
3. Assign the internal owner and competent contractor where needed.
4. Identify evidence required.
5. Link the activity to assets, locations and contractors.
6. Create recurring tasks in FM Control Hub.
7. Set escalation rules for missed checks and failed results.
8. Store completed evidence in Compliance Hub.
9. Review dashboard status monthly.
10. Update the matrix after incidents, changes or new guidance.

## Practical Matrix

| Compliance area | Typical frequency | Evidence to keep | Primary owner | Escalation trigger |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Fire alarm tests | Weekly user test, rotating call points where practicable | Fire alarm logbook entry, call point tested, faults raised | Site FM / responsible person delegate | Missed weekly test, panel fault, no audibility evidence where needed |
| Fire alarm servicing | Normally 6-monthly competent service, adjusted by risk/system | Contractor service report, defects, completion evidence | FM / fire contractor | Overdue service, critical defect, repeated false alarms |
| Emergency lighting | Monthly functional test and annual duration test | Test log, failed fittings, remedial evidence | Site FM / fire contractor | Missed test, failed escape route lighting, overdue remedials |
| Fire doors | Routine local checks plus periodic competent inspection based on risk | Fire door inspection records, defect photos, remedial close-out | FM / fire door contractor | Door not closing, damaged seals, high-risk door defect overdue |
| Fire risk assessment | Review regularly and after significant change; many sites use annual review | Current FRA, action tracker, review notes | Responsible Person / FM lead | FRA overdue, high-risk action overdue, significant change not reviewed |
| Legionella checks | Frequency set by risk assessment and written scheme | Temperature logs, flushing records, out-of-range actions, contractor reports | Responsible Person / FM | Missed flushing, out-of-range temperatures, unresolved remedials |
| Asbestos register | Keep live; reinspect known ACMs at risk-based intervals | Survey, register, management plan, reinspection records | Dutyholder / FM | Intrusive work without register check, damaged ACM, outdated survey |
| EICR | Risk-based fixed wiring inspection; commonly up to 5 years in many commercial settings | EICR, observations, remedial certificates | FM / electrical contractor | Unsatisfactory report, overdue inspection, C1/C2 defects open |
| PAT | Risk-based inspection/testing regime | Portable equipment register, inspection/test records, defects | FM / department owner | Damaged equipment in use, no regime, repeated failures |
| LOLER | Commonly 6-monthly for lifting people/accessories and 12-monthly for other lifting equipment unless written scheme states otherwise | Thorough examination reports, defect close-out | FM / competent person | Overdue thorough examination, serious defect, lift used after unsafe report |
| PUWER | Risk-based inspection and maintenance; pre-use checks for higher-risk equipment | Equipment register, inspection, maintenance, training records | FM / equipment owner | Defective equipment available, missing guarding, untrained user |
| Work at height equipment | Pre-use checks plus formal inspection at risk-based/manufacturer intervals | Ladder/access equipment register, scaffold handover, fall protection inspection | FM / contractor | Work at height without RAMS, failed equipment, no rescue plan where needed |
| COSHH assessments | Before use; review after product/process change or at least annually as local standard | Substance inventory, SDS, task COSHH assessments, training | FM / department manager | Unlabelled chemicals, no assessment, exposure incident |
| Waste duty of care | Every waste transfer; carrier/broker checks before use and periodically | Waste transfer notes, consignment notes, carrier licence checks | FM / waste contractor manager | Missing transfer note, unlicensed carrier, fly-tipping concern |
| Contractor RAMS | Before each non-routine or higher-risk task and after scope change | RAMS review record, competence, induction, permit links | FM / contract owner | Work starts without approved RAMS, generic RAMS, scope change |
| Permit to work | Before each permit-controlled task; close at task completion | Permit, isolations, fire watch, close-out evidence | Permit issuer / FM | Work starts without permit, expired live permit, permit not closed |

## FM Actions

A Facilities Manager should:

- convert the matrix into recurring tasks;
- assign every line to a named person or role;
- store evidence against the site, asset and date;
- review overdue and failed items weekly;
- separate service completion from remedial close-out;
- escalate high-risk life-safety failures immediately;
- verify contractor reports are read and actioned;
- use the matrix during audits, handovers and board reporting.

## Evidence to Keep

- Completed statutory task records.
- Contractor service reports.
- Inspection logs.
- Risk assessments and written schemes.
- Asset registers.
- Defect and remedial action trackers.
- Certificates and competent person reports.
- Permit records.
- RAMS review records.
- Waste transfer and consignment notes.
- Dashboard exports or monthly compliance reports.

## Review Frequency

- Matrix review: quarterly in active estates and after significant legal, asset or operational change.
- Dashboard review: weekly for overdue life-safety tasks, monthly for full compliance review.
- Evidence audit: sample monthly and formally audit at least annually.
- Frequency review: after incidents, contractor advice, risk assessment changes or enforcement/audit findings.

## Red Flags

- Green compliance status based only on tasks being scheduled.
- Service reports uploaded but defects not actioned.
- Logbooks kept locally with no central visibility.
- "Risk-based" used without a documented risk basis.
- Contractor portal contains evidence that FM has not reviewed.
- Missed fire, water or lifting checks not escalated.
- Statutory inspection dates depend on one person's spreadsheet.

## Longleat / Visitor Attraction Relevance

A visitor attraction needs compliance evidence that stands up during peak public operation. A missed emergency lighting check, lift defect, water hygiene failure or contractor RAMS gap can affect thousands of visitors and staff, not just a back-office team.

The matrix should support daily operational briefings, event planning, high-footfall periods, animal area infrastructure, estate roads, heritage assets and emergency planning.

## Example Scenario

The dashboard shows fire alarm servicing complete, but the contractor report includes a critical panel battery defect.

The matrix prevents false assurance. The servicing line can be marked complete, but the defect line remains open, high risk and escalated until remedial evidence is uploaded.
