## Plain English Summary

The Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012 require asbestos risks to be identified, recorded, managed and controlled. In non-domestic premises, the key FM duty is Regulation 4: the duty to manage asbestos.

For Facilities Managers, this means knowing whether asbestos-containing materials may be present, keeping an asbestos register, maintaining a management plan and making sure nobody disturbs asbestos during maintenance, refurbishment or contractor work.

## Why It Matters in Facilities Management

Asbestos is still present in many buildings built or refurbished before 2000. It becomes dangerous when fibres are released through damage, drilling, cutting, sanding or poor removal work.

FM teams control many activities that can disturb building fabric. If the asbestos register is missing, out of date or not checked before work, routine maintenance can become a serious health exposure and enforcement issue.

## Who Has Duties

- Employers.
- People with responsibility for maintenance and repair of non-domestic premises.
- Building owners, landlords, occupiers and managing agents depending on lease/control arrangements.
- Facilities Managers controlling maintenance work.
- Contractors and trades who may disturb asbestos.
- Licensed asbestos contractors where licensed work is required.

## What the Law Requires

The core requirements include:

- take reasonable steps to find out if asbestos is present;
- presume materials contain asbestos unless there is strong evidence they do not;
- assess the condition and risk from asbestos-containing materials;
- keep an up-to-date asbestos register;
- prepare and implement an asbestos management plan;
- give asbestos information to anyone liable to disturb it;
- prevent exposure or reduce it as far as reasonably practicable;
- use competent people for surveys, sampling and asbestos work;
- ensure suitable training for employees and contractors who may encounter asbestos;
- ensure licensed, notifiable and non-licensed work is managed correctly.

## FM Actions

A Facilities Manager should:

- confirm whether the premises need an asbestos management survey;
- maintain the asbestos register and make it accessible before works;
- tag or otherwise identify known asbestos risks where appropriate;
- embed asbestos checks into contractor induction, permits and RAMS review;
- stop intrusive work where asbestos information is absent or unclear;
- arrange refurbishment and demolition surveys before intrusive projects;
- inspect known asbestos-containing materials for damage or deterioration;
- escalate damaged asbestos immediately and restrict access where needed;
- keep asbestos training current for relevant staff;
- verify asbestos contractors are competent and, where needed, licensed.

## Evidence to Keep

- Asbestos management survey.
- Refurbishment and demolition surveys.
- Asbestos register.
- Asbestos management plan.
- Reinspection records.
- Contractor asbestos information issue records.
- Permit-to-work records for intrusive work.
- RAMS and method statements.
- Training records.
- Air monitoring and clearance certificates where relevant.
- Waste consignment notes for asbestos waste.
- Remedial action records and photographs.

## Review Frequency

- Asbestos management plan: review at least annually and after changes, damage or works.
- Known asbestos-containing materials: reinspect at a frequency set by risk assessment.
- Asbestos register: update whenever surveys, removals, encapsulation or condition changes occur.
- Contractor controls: check before every task that could disturb building fabric.
- Training: refresh at intervals suitable to role and exposure risk.

## Red Flags

- No asbestos survey for a pre-2000 building.
- Register exists but contractors cannot access it.
- Intrusive work starts before asbestos information is checked.
- "No asbestos" assumed without survey evidence.
- Damaged asbestos-containing material with no isolation or action.
- Contractors drilling, chasing or removing materials outside the permit process.
- Refurbishment work relying only on an old management survey.

## Interview Talking Points

Use this phrase:

> I treat asbestos as a pre-work control. Before anyone disturbs fabric, I expect the register, survey scope, RAMS and permit controls to confirm the work is safe or to stop the job until competent advice is obtained.
