## Plain English Summary

The Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 turn the broad duties of the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act into a practical management system. They require employers to assess risks, plan controls, appoint competent people, provide information and prepare for emergencies.

For FM, this is the engine room of compliance. It is where risk assessment, competent advice, monitoring and review become day-to-day management duties.

## Why It Matters in Facilities Management

Facilities Managers constantly make risk decisions. A broken fire door, failed emergency light, wet floor, blocked exit, unreliable contractor or missing Legionella log all need structured assessment.

The law expects more than common sense. It expects a documented approach that identifies hazards, evaluates risk and controls it.

## Who Has Duties

- Employers.
- Self-employed people where health and safety law applies.
- People managing risks created by their undertaking.
- Managers responsible for implementing controls.
- Competent persons appointed to assist with health and safety.

## What the Law Requires

The core requirements include:

- suitable and sufficient risk assessments;
- arrangements for effective planning, organisation, control, monitoring and review;
- health surveillance where required;
- competent assistance;
- emergency procedures;
- cooperation and coordination where multiple employers share a site;
- information and training for employees;
- special consideration for young persons, new and expectant mothers and vulnerable groups.

## FM Actions

A Facilities Manager should:

- maintain a live risk register;
- ensure each significant FM activity has a risk assessment;
- review risks after incidents, defects or operational changes;
- confirm contractors have suitable RAMS;
- coordinate risks between departments, tenants, events teams and contractors;
- maintain emergency plans;
- keep evidence of decisions and corrective actions;
- use trends to prevent repeated issues.

## Evidence to Keep

- Risk assessments.
- Risk register.
- Safe systems of work.
- Emergency plans.
- Contractor RAMS.
- Training records.
- Competent-person appointment records.
- Inspection records.
- Review minutes.
- Incident investigation reports.

## Review Frequency

- Risk assessments: annually as a baseline, sooner after incidents, changes or failures.
- Emergency arrangements: at least annually and after exercises or live incidents.
- Contractor RAMS: before works begin and whenever scope changes.
- Risk register: monthly in active FM environments.

## Red Flags

- Risk assessments copied across sites without review.
- No risk owner assigned.
- Old assessments that do not match the current building or activity.
- No escalation route for high-risk defects.
- Contractors starting work before RAMS are reviewed.
- No evidence of review after incidents.

## Interview Talking Points

Use this phrase:

> I use risk assessment as a management tool, not a paperwork exercise. The value is in identifying what can go wrong, deciding proportionate controls and proving that those controls are monitored.
