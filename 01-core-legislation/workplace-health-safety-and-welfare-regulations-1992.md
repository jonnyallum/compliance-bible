## Plain English Summary

The Workplace Health, Safety and Welfare Regulations 1992 set minimum standards for the physical workplace. They cover the basic conditions people need to work safely and with dignity: cleanliness, temperature, ventilation, lighting, toilets, washing facilities, drinking water, rest areas, safe floors, traffic routes and maintenance.

For FM, these regulations turn the building itself into a compliance responsibility.

## Why It Matters in Facilities Management

Many FM issues look small until they become legal exposure: poor lighting, slippery floors, blocked corridors, cold offices, broken toilets, inadequate ventilation or damaged surfaces.

These regulations are especially important in public-facing, care, education, office, hospitality, visitor attraction and estate environments.

## Who Has Duties

- Employers.
- People in control of workplaces.
- Landlords or managing organisations where they control workplace areas.
- Facilities or estates teams responsible for maintenance and welfare provision.

## What the Law Requires

Workplaces must be suitable for the people using them. Key areas include:

- maintenance of workplace, equipment and systems;
- ventilation;
- reasonable indoor temperature;
- suitable and sufficient lighting;
- cleanliness and waste control;
- adequate room dimensions and space;
- workstations and seating where relevant;
- safe floors and traffic routes;
- falls and falling objects precautions;
- windows and transparent doors/surfaces safety;
- toilets and washing facilities;
- drinking water;
- changing facilities where needed;
- rest and meal areas.

## FM Actions

A Facilities Manager should:

- run routine workplace inspections;
- track welfare defects and close them quickly;
- keep toilets, washrooms and rest areas usable;
- monitor heating, ventilation and lighting complaints;
- inspect floors, stairs, corridors and traffic routes;
- ensure safe pedestrian and vehicle separation where relevant;
- keep cleaning standards documented;
- maintain access, egress and welfare provisions during works.

## Evidence to Keep

- Workplace inspection checklists.
- Cleaning schedules.
- Defect logs.
- Temperature or comfort complaints and responses.
- Lighting inspection records.
- Ventilation service records.
- Washroom maintenance logs.
- Slip/trip/fall incident reports.
- Corrective action records.

## Review Frequency

- High-footfall areas: daily or weekly visual checks.
- Staff welfare areas: weekly or monthly depending on risk.
- Formal workplace inspections: monthly or quarterly.
- After layout changes, refurbishment, incidents or repeated complaints.

## Red Flags

- Broken toilets left unresolved.
- Poor lighting in public or work areas.
- Recurring slip/trip hazards.
- Poor ventilation complaints with no investigation.
- Waste build-up.
- Damaged flooring or stairs.
- No record of routine inspections.

## Interview Talking Points
