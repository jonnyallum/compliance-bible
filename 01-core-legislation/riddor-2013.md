# RIDDOR 2013

## Plain English Summary

RIDDOR means the Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013. It requires responsible persons to report certain work-related deaths, injuries, occupational diseases and dangerous occurrences to the relevant enforcing authority.

For FM, RIDDOR matters because building defects, work equipment, contractor activity, slips, trips, falls, electrical incidents, lifting operations and public-facing premises can all create reportable events.

## Why It Matters in Facilities Management

A Facilities Manager may be involved in the incident response, investigation, evidence capture and corrective actions even where another manager formally submits the report.

RIDDOR is not about blame. It is about ensuring serious work-related incidents are reported and investigated properly.

## Who Has Duties

Reports should be submitted by the responsible person, which can include:

- employers in relation to workers;
- self-employed people in certain circumstances;
- people in control of work premises when a reportable work-related accident or event has occurred.

## What Must Be Reported

Reportable categories include:

- work-related fatalities;
- specified injuries to workers;
- over-seven-day incapacitation injuries to workers;
- certain injuries to non-workers, including members of the public, where they are taken directly to hospital for treatment;
- diagnosed reportable occupational diseases;
- certain dangerous occurrences.

## FM Actions

A Facilities Manager should:

- make the scene safe;
- ensure first aid and emergency response;
- preserve evidence where appropriate;
- record what happened, where, when and who was involved;
- photograph defects or conditions where safe and appropriate;
- identify linked assets, contractors or activities;
- escalate to the responsible person or health and safety lead;
- support the RIDDOR decision;
- track corrective actions to closure;
- review related risk assessments and inspections.

## Evidence to Keep

- Accident report.
- Witness statements.
- Photographs.
- CCTV reference where available.
- Asset inspection history.
- Maintenance records.
- Contractor RAMS.
- Training records.
- Risk assessments.
- RIDDOR submission reference where reported.
- Investigation report.
- Corrective action log.

## Review Frequency

- Incident log: review monthly in normal operations.
- High-risk sites: review weekly or after every serious event.
- RIDDOR decisions: review immediately after any serious incident.
- Trend analysis: monthly or quarterly.

## Red Flags

- No accident reporting system.
- Incidents recorded but not investigated.
- Repeated slips/trips in the same area.
- Serious contractor incidents not escalated.
- No RIDDOR decision record.
- Corrective actions left open.
- Witness statements not gathered quickly.

## Interview Talking Points

Use this phrase:

> I see RIDDOR as part of a wider incident management system. The key is immediate safety, accurate reporting, evidence preservation, investigation and closing the loop so the same incident does not happen again.

## FM Control Hub Mapping

Recommended workflow:

1. Incident logged.
2. Severity selected.
3. Injured person type selected: employee, contractor, visitor, volunteer.
4. RIDDOR screening questions triggered.
5. Evidence uploaded.
6. Responsible person assigned.
7. Corrective actions created.
8. Risk assessment review scheduled.
9. Audit trail locked.

## Compliance Hub Mapping

Recommended records:

- Incident register.
- RIDDOR decision log.
- Investigation templates.
- Corrective action tracker.
- Trend dashboards.
- Evidence pack.
