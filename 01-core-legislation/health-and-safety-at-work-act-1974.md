## Plain English Summary

The Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 is the foundation of workplace health and safety law in Great Britain. It places broad duties on employers, employees, self-employed people and those who control premises to protect people from risks arising from work activities.

For Facilities Management, this is the parent law. Fire safety, work equipment, asbestos, COSHH, work at height, contractor control and risk assessment all sit beneath the same core principle: risks must be managed so far as is reasonably practicable.

## Why It Matters in Facilities Management

A Facilities Manager may not personally repair every asset, but they often control the systems, contractors, inspections, premises and evidence trail that prove the organisation is managing risk.

In a high-footfall estate, the duty extends beyond employees. Visitors, contractors, volunteers and members of the public can all be affected by FM decisions.

## Who Has Duties

- Employers.
- Employees.
- Self-employed people where applicable.
- People in control of premises.
- People managing maintenance or repair.
- Contractors and suppliers where their work creates risk.

## What the Law Requires

The Act creates general duties to:

- protect employees' health, safety and welfare;
- protect people who are not employees from risks created by work activities;
- provide safe systems of work;
- provide safe plant and equipment;
- provide information, instruction, training and supervision;
- maintain safe premises and safe access/egress;
- control dangerous substances and serious risks.

## FM Actions

A Facilities Manager should maintain a system that includes:

- risk assessments for premises, activities and contractors;
- planned preventative maintenance;
- statutory inspection schedules;
- contractor competence checks;
- accident and near-miss reporting;
- escalation routes for defects and unsafe conditions;
- documented checks for fire, water, electrical, plant and building safety;
- clear ownership of corrective actions.

## Evidence to Keep

- Health and safety policy.
- Risk assessments.
- Method statements.
- Contractor RAMS.
- Training records.
- Inspection records.
- Maintenance logs.
- Incident and near-miss reports.
- Corrective action logs.
- Audit records.
- Meeting notes showing risk decisions.

## Review Frequency

- Risk assessments: at least annually, after incidents, after significant changes, or when controls appear ineffective.
- Contractor competence: before appointment and periodically during contract.
- PPM/statutory checks: according to asset type, risk and applicable regulation.
- Incident trends: monthly or quarterly depending on site risk.

## Red Flags

- No central risk register.
- Repeated defects without closure.
- Contractors working without RAMS.
- Inspection certificates missing or expired.
- Fire or water records kept inconsistently.
- No proof of staff training.
- Reactive maintenance dominating all FM activity.

## Interview Talking Points
