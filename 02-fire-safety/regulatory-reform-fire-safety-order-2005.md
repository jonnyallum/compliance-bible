# Regulatory Reform Fire Safety Order 2005

## Plain English Summary

The Regulatory Reform Fire Safety Order 2005 is the main fire safety legislation for non-domestic premises in England and Wales. It requires the Responsible Person to assess fire risk, put suitable fire precautions in place and maintain them.

For FM, this is one of the most important compliance areas. Fire doors, alarms, emergency lighting, escape routes, signage, extinguishers, staff training and evacuation arrangements must all be actively managed.

## Why It Matters in Facilities Management

Fire safety failures can cause death, prosecution, business closure and severe reputational damage. In large public sites, visitor attractions, care settings or heritage estates, evacuation planning and maintenance evidence are critical.

The FM function often controls the assets and records that prove fire safety is being managed.

## Who Has Duties

The Responsible Person is usually the employer, owner, landlord or person in control of premises. Other people with control of premises or fire safety arrangements may also have duties.

In practice, a Facilities Manager may not be the legal Responsible Person, but may be delegated responsibility for managing fire safety systems, inspections, contractors and corrective actions.

## What the Law Requires

The Responsible Person must:

- carry out and record a fire risk assessment;
- identify people at risk;
- remove or reduce fire risks as far as reasonably practicable;
- provide and maintain fire precautions;
- plan for emergencies;
- provide information, instruction and training;
- cooperate and coordinate with others sharing the premises;
- review the fire risk assessment when circumstances change.

## FM Actions

A Facilities Manager should:

- keep the fire risk assessment current;
- track all FRA actions to closure;
- maintain fire alarm servicing records;
- test alarms according to site procedure;
- maintain emergency lighting records;
- inspect fire doors and escape routes;
- ensure extinguishers are serviced;
- monitor compartmentation defects;
- control hot works through permits;
- ensure contractors do not block exits or compromise fire safety;
- coordinate evacuation drills and lessons learned.

- Contractor fire safety controls.

## Review Frequency

Typical FM rhythm:

- Fire alarm user tests: weekly unless the competent fire strategy specifies otherwise.
- Emergency lighting functional tests: monthly.
- Emergency lighting full-duration test: annually.
- Fire doors: frequency depends on risk and building use; high-risk or high-traffic areas need more frequent checks.
- Fire risk assessment: review regularly, after significant change, after fire incidents or where there is reason to suspect it is no longer valid.

## Red Flags

- Fire risk assessment actions overdue.
- Fire doors wedged open or failing to close.
- Blocked escape routes.
- Missing emergency lighting records.
- Repeated false alarms with no investigation.
- Contractors doing hot works without permits.
- Fire alarm panel faults left unresolved.
- No evacuation plan for disabled persons or vulnerable users.
