# FM Audit Checklist Library

## Plain English Summary

This library gives Facilities Managers practical audit checklists for statutory compliance, contractor control, evidence quality and visitor attraction operations.

Each checklist is designed to produce findings, owners and evidence, not just a tick-box score.

## Audit Method

For every audit:

1. Define the site, area and scope.
2. Check the current policy, risk assessment or legal requirement.
3. Sample records and physical conditions.
4. Record compliant, non-compliant and not-applicable items.
5. Rate findings as critical, high, medium or low.
6. Assign owners and due dates.
7. Store evidence and closure proof.
8. Escalate unresolved critical or high findings.

## Finding Severity

- Critical: immediate life safety, legal breach or serious exposure risk.
- High: significant control failure requiring urgent action.
- Medium: weakness that could become significant if unmanaged.
- Low: improvement or minor documentation issue.

## 1. Fire Safety Audit

Checklist:

- Fire risk assessment current and reviewed after changes.
- Fire alarm test records complete.
- Fire alarm service records current.
- Emergency lighting monthly and annual records complete.
- Fire doors sampled and defects tracked.
- Escape routes clear.
- Fire extinguishers serviced and accessible.
- Fire action tracker has owners and dates.
- Hot works permits sampled.
- Staff fire training evidence available.

## 2. Water Hygiene Audit

Checklist:

- Legionella risk assessment current.
- Written scheme available.
- Responsible Person appointed.
- Temperature checks complete.
- Flushing records complete.
- Out-of-range readings actioned.
- Contractor reports reviewed.
- Remedials tracked to closure.
- Outlet register current.
- Low-use areas reviewed after occupancy changes.

## 3. Asbestos Management Audit

Checklist:

- Asbestos survey/register available.
- Management plan current.
- Known ACMs reinspected.
- Register checked before intrusive works.
- Contractors receive asbestos information.
- Damaged materials escalated.
- Refurbishment works have correct survey scope.
- Training evidence available.
- Waste records retained where relevant.

## 4. Electrical Safety Audit

Checklist:

- EICR current.
- EICR observations tracked.
- Electrical cupboards clear and controlled.
- Portable equipment regime risk-based.
- Contractor competence evidence available.
- Temporary supplies approved.
- Repeated trips or overheating investigated.
- Electrical permits/isolation records sampled.

## 5. LOLER Audit

Checklist:

- Lifting equipment register current.
- Thorough examinations in date.
- Passenger lift reports reviewed.
- Defects tracked to closure.
- Lifting accessories identified and inspected.
- Lift emergency communication checked.
- Maintenance records separate from thorough examination.

## 6. PUWER Audit

Checklist:

- Work equipment register current.
- Inspection/maintenance records available.
- Defective equipment quarantined.
- Guards and safety devices intact.
- Operator training records available.
- Hire equipment checked before use.
- Contractor use of site equipment controlled.

## 7. Work at Height Audit

Checklist:

- Work at height risk assessments current.
- Roof access controlled.
- Ladder register and inspections available.
- Scaffold handover/inspection records available.
- MEWP competence evidence checked.
- Fall protection inspection evidence available.
- Rescue plans in place where needed.

## 8. COSHH Audit

Checklist:

- Substance inventory current.
- Safety data sheets available.
- Task-based COSHH assessments complete.
- Chemicals labelled.
- Storage and segregation suitable.
- PPE available and used.
- Spill arrangements in place.
- Contractor substances controlled.

## 9. Waste Duty of Care Audit

Checklist:

- Waste carriers checked.
- Waste transfer notes retained.
- Hazardous waste consignment notes retained.
- Waste descriptions adequate.
- Segregation and storage suitable.
- Duty of care checks recorded.
- Waste contractor performance reviewed.

## 10. Contractor Management Audit

Checklist:

- Approved contractor register current.
- Insurance and competence evidence in date.
- RAMS reviewed before work.
- Site inductions complete.
- Permits issued where required.
- Contractor monitoring recorded.
- Non-conformance tracked.
- Contractor performance reviewed.

## 11. Permit-to-Work Audit

Checklist:

- Permit procedure current.
- Authorised issuers identified.
- Live permits visible.
- Closed permits complete.
- RAMS linked.
- Isolations recorded.
- Hot works fire watch evidence retained.
- Expired open permits escalated.

## 12. Risk Assessment Audit

Checklist:

- FM risk register current.
- Risk assessments reviewed.
- Actions have owners and dates.
- Incidents trigger review.
- High risks escalated.
- Public and visitor risks considered.
- Temporary controls monitored.

## 13. Asset Compliance Audit

Checklist:

- Asset register current.
- Critical assets identified.
- Statutory tasks linked to assets.
- Service reports reviewed.
- Defects separated from completed tasks.
- Lifecycle risks recorded.
- Missing assets investigated.

## 14. Evidence Quality Audit

Checklist:

- Evidence is dated and legible.
- Evidence links to correct site and asset.
- Reports are reviewed, not only uploaded.
- Defects converted to actions.
- Superseded documents marked clearly.
- Evidence retention period defined.
- Audit trail preserved.

## 15. Visitor Attraction FM Audit

Checklist:

- Visitor routes inspected.
- Public barriers and edges checked.
- Queue areas safe.
- Emergency access maintained.
- Event FM controls planned.
- Animal infrastructure defects escalated.
- Contractor works segregated from visitors.
- High-footfall periods considered in compliance planning.

## 16. Board Assurance Audit

Checklist:

- Monthly compliance report produced.
- Red and amber risks explained.
- Budget-blocked actions visible.
- Risk acceptance documented.
- Statutory overdue tasks escalated.
- Evidence supports dashboard status.
- Lessons learned reported after incidents.
