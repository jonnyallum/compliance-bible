## Plain English Summary

The Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations 1998 require work equipment to be suitable, maintained, inspected where needed and used only by people with adequate information, instruction and training.

For FM, PUWER applies to equipment used by employees and often overlaps with contractors, workshops, grounds equipment, access equipment, plant, machinery and maintenance tools.

## Why It Matters in Facilities Management

Facilities teams use and control a wide range of work equipment: ladders, pressure washers, floor scrubbers, drills, ride-on mowers, compactors, generators, powered gates, workshop machinery and maintenance tools.

If equipment is unsuitable, poorly maintained or used by untrained people, the result can be serious injury and clear evidence of management failure.

## Who Has Duties

- Employers.
- People and companies who own, operate or control work equipment.
- Facilities Managers providing or authorising equipment.
- Supervisors who control equipment use.
- Employees using work equipment.
- Contractors using site-owned equipment or bringing equipment onto site.

## What the Law Requires

The core requirements include:

- equipment must be suitable for the intended use and conditions;
- equipment must be maintained in an efficient state, working order and good repair;
- inspection is required where deterioration could create dangerous situations;
- users and supervisors must receive adequate information, instruction and training;
- dangerous parts of machinery must be guarded;
- controls, emergency stops, warnings and markings must be suitable;
- mobile work equipment must be managed safely;
- power presses and some higher-risk equipment have specific requirements.

## FM Actions

A Facilities Manager should:

- keep a work equipment register for FM-owned equipment;
- assign owners for inspection, maintenance and defect action;
- confirm equipment is suitable before purchase or hire;
- ensure pre-use checks are completed for higher-risk equipment;
- keep maintenance and inspection records;
- remove defective equipment from use and label it clearly;
- check guarding, emergency stops and safety devices;
- verify operator training and authorisation;
- control contractor use of site equipment;
- include PUWER checks in workshop, grounds and maintenance audits.

## Evidence to Keep

- Work equipment register.
- Purchase or hire suitability checks.
- Risk assessments and safe systems of work.
- Pre-use check records.
- Inspection and maintenance records.
- Defect and quarantine records.
- Training and authorisation records.
- Manufacturer instructions.
- Contractor equipment declarations.
- Workshop or equipment audit records.

## Review Frequency

- Pre-use checks: before use for equipment where defects could create risk.
- Formal inspections: at risk-based intervals and after events that may affect safety.
- Maintenance: in line with manufacturer guidance and risk assessment.
- Training: before use and refreshed after changes, incidents or long gaps in use.
- Equipment register: review whenever equipment is purchased, hired, modified, disposed of or found defective.

## Red Flags

- Equipment has no owner or inspection regime.
- Defective tools remain available for use.
- Guards removed or bypassed.
- Staff use equipment without training.
- Hire equipment arrives without suitability or handover checks.
- No records for high-risk equipment inspection.
- Contractor uses site equipment without permission.
- Modified equipment has not been reassessed.

## Interview Talking Points

Use this phrase:

> I treat work equipment as a controlled asset. It needs a known owner, suitable use, inspection where risk demands it, maintenance evidence and trained users.
