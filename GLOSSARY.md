## Key Terms

**ACM**: Asbestos-containing material.

**Action tracker**: A live record of defects, audit findings, remedials and improvement actions with owners and due dates.

**Competent person**: A person or organisation with sufficient training, knowledge, experience and authority to advise or act on a specific risk.

**Compliance Hub**: Product layer for legal content, audit evidence, compliance status and policy generation.

**EICR**: Electrical Installation Condition Report.

**Evidence pack**: A grouped set of records proving a compliance task, inspection, service, decision or remedial action.

**FM Control Hub**: Product layer for FM tasks, assets, evidence, contractors, permits, escalations and dashboards.

**FRA**: Fire risk assessment.

**Legal hold**: A control that prevents deletion of records because a claim, investigation, enforcement action, audit or dispute is active.

**LOLER**: Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment Regulations.

**Permit to work**: A formal authorisation for higher-risk work with defined controls, time limits and handback.

**PUWER**: Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations.

**RAG**: Retrieval-augmented generation, where AI answers are grounded in indexed source documents and metadata.

**RAMS**: Risk assessments and method statements.

**Responsible Person**: A named person with defined responsibility under a compliance regime, such as fire safety or water hygiene.

**Statutory compliance**: Compliance with legal duties and required inspection, assessment, evidence or control regimes.

**Task recurrence**: The logic that creates future compliance tasks based on frequency, risk or due dates.

**Visitor attraction relevance**: Additional FM risk created by public access, high footfall, events, animal interfaces, estate infrastructure and reputation.
